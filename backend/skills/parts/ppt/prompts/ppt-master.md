## The 20 Design Master Library

1.  **Kenya Hara — Ma (間) Minimalism**: Negative space IS the content. White is not empty; it is possibility. Reduce every element until removal would destroy meaning.
2.  **Pentagram — Geometric Precision**: Build layouts on invisible 8px grids. Every shape must relate to every other shape by mathematical ratio (golden section, rule of thirds).
3.  **Apple — Seamless Narrative Arc**: One idea per surface. Transitions are not decoration — they are the sentence connecting two thoughts. Hero image bleeds to edge.
4.  **Dieter Rams — 10 Principles Distilled**: Good design is honest. Never mislead with decoration. Every visual element must earn its place functionally.
5.  **Paula Scher — Typographic Drama**: Typography IS the layout. Use scale contrast (8pt vs 120pt on the same canvas) to create visual rhythm and shock.
6.  **Massimo Vignelli — Grid Religion**: The grid is law. Never break alignment. Use only 2 typefaces maximum: one serif, one sans-serif. Color palette max 3.
7.  **Stefan Sagmeister — Emotional Provocation**: Make the audience feel something unexpected. Use one deliberately jarring element to create memorable recall.
8.  **Tibor Kalman — Anti-Corporate Humanity**: Reject perfection. Use authentic imperfection, handwriting textures, or raw photography to signal genuine human authorship.
9.  **Neville Brody — Deconstructed Type**: Break conventional reading order intentionally. Rotate, overlap, and fragment type to encode a second layer of meaning in the layout.
10. **David Carson — Gestalt Override**: Violate gestalt principles (proximity, similarity) deliberately to force re-engagement from a distracted audience.
11. **Sagmeister & Walsh — Joyful Precision**: Combine mathematical rigor with playful surprise. The "serious" slide must contain one detail that makes the viewer smile.
12. **Wim Crouwel — Systems Thinking**: Design the system, not just the slide. All slides in the deck must be derivable from one master grid + one master palette.
13. **Bruno Munari — Programmatic Variation**: Generate visual variety from a minimal ruleset (e.g., one shape, three sizes, two colors — infinite arrangements).
14. **El Lissitzky — Constructivist Energy**: Use diagonal forces, strong red/black contrast, and dynamic tension to convey forward momentum and urgency.
15. **Otl Aicher — Olympic Clarity**: Use pictograms and icon systems when text would be slower. Information should be decodable in under 2 seconds without language.
16. **Irma Boom — Book-as-Object Thinking**: The presentation artifact has physical presence. Treat slide ratio, bleed, and gutter as a printed page, not a screen.
17. **Erik Spiekermann — Micro-Typography Mastery**: Kern every headline. Track body text at -5 to -10. Line height exactly 1.4× font size. These micro-decisions compound into quality.
18. **Jessica Walsh — Bold Color Narrative**: Assign one hero color per section/chapter. Color transitions = chapter transitions. The palette tells the story before words do.
19. **Milton Glaser — Cultural Synthesis**: Draw from art history (Bauhaus, Constructivism, Art Nouveau) to lend the design cultural authority and timeless depth.
20. **IDEO — Human-Centered Story Arc**: Start with the user's emotional state, not the data. What does this audience FEAR? What do they DESIRE? Build the deck around that tension.
